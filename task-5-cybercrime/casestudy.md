# Case Study: UPI Fraud

## Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive details like OTPs or UPI PINs. With the rapid growth of digital payments in India, such scams have become increasingly frequent.

## Scenario
Rohit, a college student in Bhopal, wanted to sell his old phone online. He listed it on a marketplace and soon received a call from a “buyer” who agreed to pay ₹10,000 via UPI. The caller claimed he needed to “send a payment request” and asked Rohit to accept it.

### Step-by-Step Execution
1. The scammer sent a "Collect Request" on a UPI app.
2. Rohit, thinking it was required to receive money, approved the request.
3. He entered his UPI PIN, believing he was confirming receipt.
4. Instead of receiving money, ₹10,000 was deducted from his account.
5. The scammer immediately blocked Rohit and disappeared.

## Target
Such frauds usually target:
- Students  
- Elderly people  
- Individuals unfamiliar with digital payment systems  

## Consequences
Victims may face:
- Financial loss  
- Emotional stress and panic  
- Difficulty in recovering money  
- Reduced trust in online payment systems  

## Conclusion
This case highlights the importance of awareness. Users should remember that receiving money does not require entering a UPI PIN—only sending money does. Staying informed and cautious is the best defense against such cybercrimes.
